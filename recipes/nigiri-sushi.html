Nigiri Sushi

Description

Nigiri-sushi is the Japanese name for sushi where, for example, raw fish
or prawns are placed on oval cushions of sushi rice. Make a batch of sushi
rice and add your favorite toppings. The sushi should be served cold,
preferably with soy, wasabi and ginger.

Ingredients

Sushi rice

  • 4 dl of round grain rice (preferably Japanese sushi rice)
  • 4 dl water
  • 4 tablespoons of rice vinegar

Proteins

  • 140 grams of fish fillet preferably salmon or fresh tuna (which has
    been frozen for 3-5 days)
  • 1/2 batch of omelette
  • Shrimps

Steps

Sushi rice

  1. Place the rice in a saucepan. Rinse the rice until the water looks clear.
    Pour it off. Add water. Leave for 30 minutes.
  2. Stir the rice. Boil the water. Cover with a lid, reduce the heat to the
    lowest possible. Simmer for 20 minutes.
  3. Remove the pan from the heat. Leave for 10-20 minutes
    without lifting the lid.
  4. Place the rice in a large porcelain dish. Sprinkle the rice vinegar over
    the rice. Cut slits in the rice with a spatula or large spoon. Carefully
    mix the vinegar with the rice without mashing the rice grains.

After the rice is finished

  1. Cut the fish into 3-4 mm thick slices, preferably rectangles,
    approx. 3x6 cm.
  2. Form small egg-shaped balls of the rice with moistened hands.
  3. Spread a thin layer of wasabi on the rice ball and top with a slice
    of fish.
Tip

Nigiri are oblong balls of sushi rice with a small dollop of wasabi and on
top a thin slice of fish, or shellfish tail. The rice is shaped with well
-moistened hands. Make the nigiri sushi just before serving.
