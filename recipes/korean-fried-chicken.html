Korean Fried Chicken

Description

Korean fried chicken, also called chikin, is a recipe for Korean chicken that
is fried and then dipped in a delicious sauce with honey, rice vinegar and
gochujang. Serve the fried chicken with quick pickled vegetables. Comfort
food in Korean!

Ingredients

Quick pickled vegetables

  • 1 dl rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon powdered sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 carrot
  • 10cm of black radish
  • 1/2 red onion

The sauce

  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 1 tablespoon neutral oil
  • 1/2 dl of Japanese soy
  • 2-4 tablespoons of gochujang
  • 1/2 dl honey
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 dl brown sugar
  • 3 tablespoons of ketchup

Frying batter

  • 1 & 1/2 dl of flour
  • 1 & 1/2 dl cornstarch
  • 1 & 1/2 teaspoons of baking Soda
  • 3 dl of ice cold water

Chicken

  • 2 & 1/2 dl cornstarch
  • 2 & 1/2 teaspoons of garlic powder
  • 2 teaspoons of gochugaru, Korean chili
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 600 grams of chicken thigh fillet
  • 1 & 1/2 liters of neutral oil (for frying)

Steps

Quick pickled vegetables

  • Mix rice vinegar, sugar and salt until the sugar dissolves in a bowl
    that also holds the vegetables. Peel and shred the carrot and chop
    thinly. Peel and shred the red onion. Mix the vegetables into the
    mixture. Let stand 30 minutes

The sauce

  • Peel and finely chop the garlic and fry soft oil in a small pan. Add
    the other ingredients and let it boil for 10–15 minutes, uncovered,
    until a sticky sauce forms. Then put aside.

Frying batter

  • Whisk all the ingredients together into a smooth batter.

Chicken

  1. Mix cornstarch, garlic powder, gochugaru and salt in a bowl.
  2. Cut each chicken thigh fillet into 3 pieces. First turn the chicken
    in the cornstarch and then in the batter so they are covered all
    around. Then fold in the cornstarch once more.
  3. Heat the oil to 180°C in a large saucepan.
  4. Fry the chicken in batches for about 10 minutes or until the
    chicken is cooked through and crispy. Place on a wire rack or
    kitchen paper and let drain. If you want the chicken extra crispy,
    fry it once more.
For serving
  • Put the chicken in a bowl and mix with the sauce so the chicken is
    covered all around, sprinkle with sesame seeds and peanuts.
